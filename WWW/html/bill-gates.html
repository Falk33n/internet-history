    BILL GATES

    Bill Gates is the creator of Microsoft and is one of the wealthiest men on this planet. Microsoft was founded back in 1975 and created by Gats himself and his college friend Paul Allen. Gates saw early on the potential of personal computers (PC's) , he was a talented businessman who made Microsoft a powerhouse in the market. Gates has a wife named Melinda. He and his wife have donated billions of dollars to charity. In this section you will learn more about the big name "Bill Gates". [1]

    BACKGROUND


    William Henry Gates was born on the 28th of October 1955 in Washington. Gates grew up in an upper-middle-class family and had an older brother and a younger sister, his father, William H. Gates II, and his mother, Mary Gates, who was a school teacher and eventually became a homemaker. [1]

    THE EVOLUTION OF A TECH PIONEER

    Gates oversaw Microsoft's transformation from a startup to a global technology giant. Gates leads the race for being the best-known entrepreneur for the personal computers indusry. Forbes magazine has ranked Bill as the richest man on earth for several years. What many people don't know is that Gates has actually also released several books.

    Gates went to public elementary school and, later, on, the private Lakeside School in Seattle, US. In eighth grade, Lakeside's administrators discovered that Gates had hacked the school computer system to get free time on the mainframe. He was later arrested for this and spent a whole night in jail. Gates has later on said that this event was "the single biggest influence on my career". [1]

    FROM HARVARD DROPOUT TO TECH ICON

    Gates graduated from Lakeside back in 1973 and enrolled at Harvard University. Though, two years later, he dropped out, to pursue his business career. In 1975, Gates and Paul Allen co-founded Microsoft in Alburerque, New Mexico, US. The first product they developed was a version of the BASIC programming language for the Altair 8800. The Altair was one of the first commercially available microcomputers on the market. In 1980, they released its MS-DOS operating system for IBM's personal computers. This was a major breakthrough for Microsoft, since IBM's PCs became the industry standard.


    Microsoft continued to release new versions of MS-DOS and Windows, a graphical interface operating system. Windows became large in popularity and by the mid-1990s,Windows was running on more than 90% of the world's PCs. In 2000 Gates stepped down as CEO of Microsoft, but he remained chairman and chief software architect. In 2006, Gates announced that he would step out of his day-to-day role and focus instead on philanthropy. He went on to find the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, which to this date, is the largest private charitable foundation on earth.

    Gates is one of the most successful business people of all time. Gates have changed the technology industry for good. He is an inspiration for entrepreneurs all over the world. [1]

    PHILANTHROPY, ADVOCACY, AND GLOBAL IMPACT

    In recent years, Gates has increased his involvement in philanthropy. He has pledged to give away most of his fortune to charitable causes. In 2010, Gates and Warren Buffet created the Giving Pledge, a campaign to encourage other billionaires to also, just like Gates, donate the majority of their wealth to charity. As of 2019, over 200 billionares have signed the Giving Pledge.


    In addition to the Gates foundation, Gates has also been active in global health initiatives. He serves on boards of several organizations, including the GAVI Alliance and the Global Fund to Fight AIDS, Tuberculosis, and Malaria. Bill has been criticized for his involvement in some of these organizations, particularly the GAVI Alliance, since that organization has been accused of promoting vaccination programs in developing countries that are not based on scientific evidence. [1]

    FAILURES


    Here are some of Bill Gates' biggest failures: [1]

    1. Microsoft Zune:
      Microsoft launched the Zunue back in 2006 and it was a total flop. The Zune was bulky, had a short battery life, and a clunky user interface. The Zune was discontinued in 2011.

    2. Windows Vista:
      Vista was released back in 2007 to much funfare. However, users and reviewers were quick to pan it. Vista was buggy, slow, and full of security vulnerabilities. The result of this made many users stuck with the old Windows XP operating system instead. Windows Vista is seen as a major failure for Microsoft.

    3. Microsoft Kin:
      In 2010, Kin was released, a smartphone aimed at teenagers. The Kin had a poor selection of apps, it was overpriced, and had a confusing user interface. The Kin was discontinued after just 6 weeks on the market.

    4. Windows 8:
      Windows 8 was released in 2012. It contained a major redesign of the Windows operating system, and it was not well-received by users. They found the new "Metro" user interface difficult and confusing to use. This led to many users being stuck with the older version of Windows 7.

    5. Microsoft Surface:
      In 2012, Microsoft released the Surface, a tablet computer. It was a total flop. It had a poor selection of apps, it was overpriced, and also had a confusing user interface. Microsoft has discontinued the Surface ever since.

    6. Windows 10 Mobile:
      In 2015, Microsoft released Windows 10 Mobile, an operating system for smartphones. It was a total flop. It had a poor selection of apps, it was overpriced, and also had a confusing user interface. Microsoft has discontinued Windows 10 Mobile ever since.

    7. Microsoft HoloLens:
      In 2016, Microsoft released the HoloLens, a mixed reality headset. It was a total flop. The HoloLens was bulky, had a short battery life, and a clunky user interface. Microsoft has discontinued the HoloLens ever since.

    8. Microsoft Band:
      In 2014, Microsoft released Microsoft Band, a fitness tracker. It was a total flop. The Microsoft Band had a short battery life, was bulky, and a clunky user interface. Microsoft has discontinued the Microsoft Band ever since.

    9. Windows 10:
      In 2015, Microsoft released Windows 10, an operating system for tablets and PCs. It was a flop. It had a confusing user interface, it was buggy, and full of privacy-invading features. This led to many users being stuck with the older version of Windows 7.

    10. Microsoft Edge:
      In 2015, Microsoft released Edge, a web browser. It was a total flop. Microsoft Edge had a confusing user interface, a poor selection of features and it was buggy. This led to many users being stuck with the older Internet Explorer web browser. Microsoft has discontinued the Edge ever since.

    A LOOK BACK AT PREDICTIONS AND MISSES


    In an interview back in 1995, Gates predicted that, within a decade, people would regularly watch TV shows, movies and other entertainment online. In fact, 10 years later, in 2005, YouTube was created. Two years later, Netflix was founded.

    Gates missed the mark when the interviewer suggested that the internet could become a rifle of misinformation that could easily spread to large amounts of people. Bill Gates was doubtful that the internet would become a repository for what might now be known as "fake news". He argued that having more opportunities to verify information by authorities would balance out the spread of misinformation. [2]
